Assumptions and Limitations of Simplified Model

  • Model excludes government or state Age Pension because tax concessions require personalized inputs and a more complex tax model.
  • Fund Net Growth Rate assumes any investment returns, dividends or capital gains are directly reinvested in the year the gain was made.
  • Model excludes any additional or ongoing income or investment sources.
  • Consistent average inflation and fund growth throughout the investment and retirement period.
  • Reduced mobility later in life will slightly reduce annual living expenses.
Inflation Rate
Inflation has remained within the Reserve Bank of Australia's target inflation rate of 2 to 3 percent for over twenty five years.
Fund Net Growth Rate
The net growth of funds including dividends and capital gains less fees and taxes. As a baseline comparison the S&P 500 Index has an average growth of ~12% per year excluding fees and taxes. Assume a 50% capital gains tax * 34.5% marginal tax rate = Net growth rate ~ 10%
Reduced Mobility Expenses
The Association of Superannuation Funds of Australia Limited (ASFA) estimate expenses for retirees aged 85 and over are approx 7% lower than those aged 65, because over 85 years any increased medical expenses are offset by significant reduction in travel and leisure expenses.
